<h2>Incorporation Process: A Step-by-Step Guide</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Name Search and Reservation</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Conduct a Name Search:</strong> Visit the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) online portal to verify that your proposed company name is available.</li>
  <li><strong>Reserve the Name:</strong> If the name is available, reserve it immediately by paying the nominal reservation fee (included in the first million cost).</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Submit at least two name options to improve approval chances.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Preparation of Documents</h3>
<p>Gather the essential documentation:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Identification Documents:</strong> Passport copies or national IDs for all directors and shareholders.</li>
  <li><strong>Registered Office Proof:</strong> Evidence of your Nigerian office address.</li>
  <li><strong>Memorandum and Articles of Association (MEMART):</strong> Outlines company objectives, governance structure, and internal regulations.</li>
  <li><strong>Share Capital Details:</strong> Provide the proposed share capital and shareholder breakdown.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Step 3: Payment of Fees and Filing</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Payment Process:</strong> Submit the fee corresponding to your company's share capital.</li>
  <li><strong>Filing:</strong> Upload all required documents along with your proof of payment on the CAC portal.</li>
  <li><strong>Certificate of Incorporation:</strong> Once approved, the CAC will issue your Certificate of Incorporation.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Step 4: Post-Incorporation Compliance</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Tax Registration:</strong> Update the Tax Identification Number (TIN) on TaxPromax for all tax-related activities.</li>
  <li><strong>VAT Registration:</strong> Register for Value Added Tax if applicable to your business.</li>
  <li><strong>Corporate Bank Account:</strong> Open a bank account using your Certificate of Incorporation and TIN.</li>
  <li><strong>Ongoing Compliance:</strong> File annual returns and update any changes with the CAC to remain in good standing.</li>
</ul>
