<p>Did you know that if you register your brand as a Business Name or Enterprise, you actually need both FIRS TIN and JTB TIN to register on TaxPro Max? Most CAC agents don't even know about this requirement.</p>

<p>If you've already registered your business or company and have gotten your TIN, you need to verify your CAC/TIN on TaxPro Max and complete your activation. This is a critical step that many business owners miss, and it can affect your ability to file taxes and access certain business services.</p>

<h2>What is TaxPro Max?</h2>

<p>TaxPro Max is the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) official online platform for tax registration, filing, and management in Nigeria. Every registered business needs to be properly onboarded on TaxPro Max to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>File annual tax returns</li>
  <li>Obtain Tax Clearance Certificates</li>
  <li>Comply with federal tax regulations</li>
  <li>Access government contracts and grants</li>
  <li>Open corporate bank accounts with certain banks</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Missing Link: Why Most Agents Get It Wrong</h2>

<p>When you register your business with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), you automatically receive a TIN. However, many agents stop there. What they don't tell you is that your TIN needs to be properly activated and verified on the TaxPro Max portal.</p>

<p>Most business owners register their companies, receive their certificate, and think they're done. But without proper TaxPro Max activation, you are technically not fully compliant with FIRS regulations. This can lead to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Penalties for non-filing (even if you didn't know you needed to file)</li>
  <li>Inability to get Tax Clearance Certificates when needed</li>
  <li>Issues with banks requiring proof of tax compliance</li>
  <li>Problems bidding for government or corporate tenders</li>
</ul>
