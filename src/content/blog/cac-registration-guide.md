<h2>Why Register with the CAC?</h2>
<p>Registration isn't just about a piece of paper; it's about <strong>Business Enablement</strong>.</p>
<ul>

  <li><strong>Banking:</strong> You can open a corporate bank account.</li>
  <li><strong>Credibility:</strong> Customers and investors trust registered brands over informal ones.</li>
</ul>

<h2>7 Steps to Register Your Business</h2>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Name Search:</strong> Choose a unique name and reserve it on the CAC portal.</li>
  <li><strong>Account Creation:</strong> Register on the CAC post-incorporation portal.</li>
  <li><strong>Fill the Forms:</strong> Input details of Directors, Shareholders, and the Registered Address.</li>
  <li><strong>Document Upload:</strong> Attach IDs (NIN/International Passport) and signatures.</li>
  <li><strong>Payment:</strong> Settle the filing fees and Stamp Duty via Remita.</li>
  <li><strong>Review:</strong> Wait for the CAC registrar to approve your application.</li>
  <li><strong>Certification:</strong> Download your Certificate of Incorporation and Status Report.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</h2>

<h3>1. Can I register my business myself?</h3>
<p>Yes, the CAC portal is open to the public. However, for Companies (LTD), errors in your <strong>Articles of Association</strong> can cause expensive delays. That's where <strong>Vobels Limited</strong> provides expert guidance. We also provide NSR TIN (onboarded on Taxpromax).</p>

<h3>2. How long does it take?</h3>
<p>Typically, a Business Name takes 2-5 working days, while a Limited Company takes 7-14 days, depending on name approval speed.</p>

<h3>3. What comes after CAC registration?</h3>
<p>Once registered, you must obtain your <strong>Tax Identification Number (TIN)</strong> from the FIRS and register for <strong>VAT</strong> if you are selling taxable goods or services. Vobels effortlessly handles this process.</p>
